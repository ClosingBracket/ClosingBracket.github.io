CS371p Fall 2020: Tony Chang - Week 8

CS371p Fall 2020: Tony Chang #

What did you do this past week?

This past week got the chance to make significant progress on the Allocator project we have in OOP and have now mostly finished the project. Additionally, I also studied plenty and constructed a page of notes for the OOP exam. Then, I took the two-part exam on Wednesday and Thursday night!

What’s in your way?

I actually don’t think anything is currently in my way. I only need to tidy up a few things on my Allocator project before submitting it finally.

What will you do next week?

This week, I need to spend less time focusing on OOP and on the projects and tasks I need to do for my other classes. For example, I currently have a take-home exam in another class that I need to work on, and also the various projects and homeworks I need to finish. Beyond that, I need to continue my recruiting search and work on more HackerRanks and such.

If you read it, what did you think of The Liskov Substitution Principle?

Yes, I did have an opportunity to read it and I think it most certainly makes sense. I believe it is a follow-up to the previous week’s reading, because it mentioned the Object-Oriented Design article that we previously read. I think that what it covered is true. The example of the square inheriting from the rectangle because it makes sense mathematically but breaks down in programming behavior was a fantastic example of how we can apply the principle. Intuitively, I’d also make the square inherit from the rectangle but I understand how the behaviors differ and would cause the invariants that a programmer might make about a rectangle break down.

What was your experience of Test #1?

I thought test 1 was okay. The concepts covered on the exam such as accumulate or the iterators were familiar to me because of what we had covered in class up to this point. I certainly do now realize how incredibly unhelpful and verbose C++'s compilation errors are in comparison to other languages’, such as Python or Java. I definitely did spend more of my time on the HackerRanks trying to debug quirky compilation errors rather than developing actual code, simply by applying keywords like consts or taking them out in certain places.

What made you happy this week?

My current desk is pretty small and doesn’t give me much room to have notes, a keyboard, my monitors, and my mouse on. However, I recently discovered a larger table lying around my house that I’m very excited to switch out to! I think the extra table real estate will be very nice to have.

What is your pick-of-the-week?

My pick of the week this week is Hugo! Hugo is a static HTML site generator that allows anybody to super easily make their own website. The website you are reading this blog on was created using Hugo, and I didn’t have to bother with any HTML at all. I am simply just typing my blog thoughts into a Markdown file and having Hugo take the contents of this file and transforming it into the appropriate HTML and CSS! It makes it incredibly easy to make your own website.
